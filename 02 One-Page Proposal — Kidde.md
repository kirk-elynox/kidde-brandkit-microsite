**KIDDE AI ENABLEMENT DISCOVERY ENGAGEMENT**

*A six-week, fixed-scope path from a scored use-case shortlist to a working prototype and a durable data-foundation plan.*

**TARGET:** To move Kidde from its established Copilot foundation to a proven, specialized AI use case within six weeks, selected on evidence and built for real adoption.

- To triage Kidde's gathered use-case backlog on organizational maturity, AI fit, and economics, so effort concentrates on the highest-value candidates.
- To deliver one working rapid prototype that the intended frontline users have actually put their hands on.
- To produce a scoped read of the data foundation the prototype relies on, informing Kidde's broader data-management strategy.
- To leave Kidde's AI Center of Excellence with a documented triage-and-prototype method it can adopt.

Kidde has stood up its AI foundation, with a broad Microsoft 365 Copilot rollout, supporting training, and a newly formed AI Center of Excellence, and is now turning toward specialized use cases while working to put the right data-management strategy in place. Elynox specializes in exactly this transition, leaning out knowledge-work processes by integrating AI where it earns its place and steering away from large technology commitments until a prototype has proven the value. Daniel Englebretson, who leads delivery, has run this pattern inside manufacturers, standing up working prototypes in five-day cycles and prioritizing use cases by team readiness and economic return. Elynox is deliberately a small, senior team rather than a large integrator. A short, fixed-scope discovery phase converts the existing backlog into a ranked shortlist, ships one prototype the users can touch, and returns a clear recommendation for where enablement pays off next, without a premature platform commitment.

**FINANCIAL:** The proposed Phase 1 spans six weeks across three two-week sprints, offered at a fixed monthly investment in the range of $5,000 to $7,500. It is scoped as a first phase designed to earn a continuing enablement retainer, not to require one. No costs beyond the stated fee are anticipated for Phase 1.
